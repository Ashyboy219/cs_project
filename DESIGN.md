## Consolidated Build Plan
# JUN-WORLD — Consolidated Build Plan

## 1. Vision Lock

JUN-WORLD is a top-down, 4-direction, procedurally-rendered pygame story game where a scared but kind outsider, dropped into the tyrant Sufflok's dimension, fights to get home and is pulled into toppling a structure of fear. The pivot turns a forgiving stealth tutorial into a **tense, punishing, save-or-reload survival adventure** built on one crunchy core verb — the **i-frame dash through a telegraphed threat** — wrapped in projectile combat, resource scarcity, and system-driven bosses you out-think rather than out-DPS. Every mechanic routes into three tracked moral leanings (MERCY / SURVIVAL / CRUELTY) that change *how* you play, not just what a menu says; the same Z-prompt that spares can kill, and the world remembers. We ship a **complete 3-act spine first** (Villages → Kingdom → Prison + portal-reveal finale, one ending per path), proven act-by-act against a real fun bar, with Theater and Underground as gated expansion. Elegance over accumulation: a finished smaller game beats an abandoned epic.

## 2. Canonical Act List (pacing fixes folded in)

The pacing critique drives three hard changes, baked into the list below: **(a)** Act 1 onboarding is flattened — verbs arrive one at a time with isolated no-stakes practice before any boss demands them; **(b)** Act 4's rhythm/beat mechanic is replaced with a dt-safe **Simon-says pattern-memory** variant; **(c)** every act is independently shippable so pacing is validated as you go. Acts 4–5 are **STRETCH** (see §6).

### ACT 1 — The Ruined Gate *(MUST — this is the vertical slice)*
- **Zone:** Ruined Villages (warm dusk; ambient ×0.92).
- **Headline mechanic:** **Dash with i-frames + HP/death + save/reload.** Verbs taught one at a time: move/talk → HP (Nervous Villager beat) → save shrine (the well) → Sling (one projectile) → **isolated harmless dash corridor (Rook tosses pebbles you dash through — zero death)** → only then the chase boss.
- **Boss:** **The Tax-Wagon & The Collector** — a scrolling-lane CHASE boss. Cannot be out-DPS'd at first; taught as dodge/weave. Phase 2 lets you turn and fight at a dead end.
- **Key puzzle:** **Cart-Roadblock** (sokoban-lite: push 2 barrels into gullies to clear the road) + **Counting Well** (bell-lever pattern-memory opening the save shrine cache).
- **Moral branching:** Collector spared by *reading the ledger names* (Mercy, harder), baited into his own road-cracks (Survival), or overpowered + wagon commandeered (Cruelty, villagers later flinch). Sets persistent `ankam_disposition`. Closing raid-escape teaches dashing past sightlines under pressure.

### ACT 2 — The Theatrical Crown *(MUST)*
- **Zone:** Twisted Castle Kingdom (tarnished red/gold; ambient ×0.88).
- **Headline mechanic:** **Full projectile combat + item/weapon inventory + shop economy.** Enemies shoot telegraphed volleys; dash-dodge through them using pillars as cover. Weird Shopkeeper introduced (chits currency, Sling→Whipcord upgrade).
- **Boss:** **Mr. Ankam, Master of Ceremonies** — a PUZZLE boss (Simon-says panel sequence + spotlight-stealth + shockwave-ring dodge; a "Composure" meter, no HP).
- **Key puzzle:** **Rigged Decree / Court Pressure-Plates** (cross the hall on gold tiles only; exploit the law's wording) + **Mirror Gallery** (light-routing using the raycast engine).
- **Moral branching:** Expose Ankam's fear → recruit informant (Mercy); win the rigged game flawlessly → forged castle pass key-item (Survival); seize the stage / intimidate → Sufflok flags you as rival, traumatized courtier becomes the fearful Servant (Cruelty).

### ACT 3 — The Cold Ledger *(MUST — spine finale lands here)*
- **Zone:** Prison Zones (cold blue; ambient ×0.80). **Stealth-primary zone** (guns = last-resort panic button).
- **Headline mechanic:** **Upgraded stealth + keycard gating + lever/switch routing + scripted follow-escort** (NOT panic-AI: follower walks a fixed path, pauses when you stop). Hard resource scarcity (no shop).
- **Boss:** **Warden Oblisk, the Counting Eye** — a SYSTEM boss: never in range; you reach/disable 3 relay panels through a gate-maze under fire (sokoban crate-on-plate).
- **Key puzzle:** **Cell-Block Key Relay** (linked-toggle levers route an open-gate "wave" past sweeping cones) + **Intake Conveyor** dash gauntlet.
- **Moral branching:** Open every cell to dissolve Oblisk's authority (Mercy); forge your own release via the manifest (Survival); shatter the console, freeing something contained below (Cruelty). **Portal reveal:** ledger page — Sufflok fears outsiders who came through doors *before* you. Former Castle Servant confirms Ankam's fear is real.
- **Truncated spine ending:** After Act 3, a compressed finale beat delivers the portal reveal + one ending per moral path so the MUST game is *complete* even if Acts 4–5 never ship.

### ACT 4 — Applause, On Pain Of *(STRETCH)*
- **Zone:** Forced Theater. **Combat-primary** at the boss; performance-stealth elsewhere.
- **Headline mechanic:** **Simon-says loyalty pageant + disguise** (NOT beat-timing). Pattern-memory + between-pattern sabotage windows. Crowd-sentiment meter.
- **Boss:** **The Ringmaster / Masked Prima** — pattern-memory + telegraphed bullet spreads; mercy "match the routine," survival "break props," cruelty "upstage."
- **Key puzzle:** **Simon-Says Pageant** (with secret banned-folk-dance subversion) + **Heckler's Script** (timed branching dialogue duel resolving Ankam's arc).
- **Moral branching:** Ankam's entire fate resolves (defector / broken / terrified-informant), changing the finale.

### ACT 5 — The Door That Lies *(STRETCH)*
- **Zone:** Nightmare Underground (near-black; ambient ×0.65). Portal mystery payoff.
- **Headline mechanic:** **Doors-that-lie looping rooms with a single fixed, pre-taught honest cue + guaranteed fallback exit after N loops** (de-risked from non-Euclidean). Lantern reveals true doors.
- **Boss:** **The Riddle-King / Looping Warden of Doors** — riddle-gated, answers drawn from your own run; "greatest hits" mechanic test; mirror-self of your dominant path.
- **Key puzzle:** **Lying Doors** + **Riddle Creature's Three Gates** + **Sliding-Sigil** ice-slide.
- **Moral branching:** Answer with truth → lore + ally (Mercy); map the loop (Survival); break the doors → damages the home portal, forecloses the clean ending (Cruelty). "Go home now vs finish it" branch.
- **Fairness rule (critique fix):** the honest cue (lantern-lit / pulse-in-time-with-hum) is introduced in a **zero-stakes entrance room and named once by the Riddle Creature before any damage is possible.** The threat stays un-masterable; the *rule* is always fair.

### ACT 6 — The Outsider's Crown *(MUST as truncated finale; STRETCH as full version)*
- **Zone:** Sufflok's Castle.
- **Headline mechanic:** **Synthesis** — all prior mechanics interleaved; ally-assist by record; branching approach (assault / infiltrate / parley).
- **Boss:** **Sufflok** — multi-phase (ordered grid bullet-hell → cracking platforms → bare desperate throne), branching per dominant path.
- **Key puzzle:** **Ledger Throne** (lights-out parity to drop his shield) + **Portal Heart** (final mirror-routing = the literal ending choice).
- **Moral branching:** Refuse the throne / parley (Mercy), outlast & escape through the portal (Survival), take the crown (Cruelty), plus early-exit and neutral endings.

## 4. Per-System Definition of Done

| System | Done when… |
|---|---|
| **S0 state refactor** | `new_game_state()` produces a fresh run; `apply_save(data)` restores attrs then `load_scene(scene,spawn)` and overrides player tile. Old `respawn` tuple + Checkpoint save-behavior deleted. |
| **S1 saves** | Autosave fires on every `_do_transition` writing `auto` slot with an "Autosaved" toast; 3 manual slots overwrite with confirm; corrupt JSON → "Save corrupted", slot disabled, no crash; `SAVE_VERSION` mismatch refused gracefully; save blocked during combat/cutscene/chase ("Can't save now"). |
| **S2 ListMenu** | One widget renders title/load/save/pause/gameover with label+sublabel+enabled, W/S/↑↓ nav, Z/Enter confirm, shared nav SFX; reused unchanged in ≥3 modes. |
| **S3 HP** | `hurt(amt,src)` respects i-frames, applies knockback via `_axis`, sets hurt_flash, screenshake, plays sound; HP bar draws; HP≤0 → GameOver mode (not respawn); heal clamps to max; serialized. |
| **S4 dash** | Tap dash key → fixed-distance dash in facing dir, ~0.16s i-frames, ~0.55s cd (difficulty-scaled), passes enemies but not walls, motion trail + whoosh; cd pip on HUD; never conflicts with Shift-sneak. |
| **S5 difficulty** | Every raw combat constant reads `S.DIFFICULTY[...]`; STORY/HAUNTED(default)/CRUEL pick on New Game; choice serialized; the §7 target numbers are in the table. |
| **S6 projectiles** | `shots` list updates: moves, despawns on solid via `is_solid_px`, hits enemies/player through `hurt()`, optional pierce; inserted into y-sorted draw_list (never blitted on top). |
| **S7 weapons** | ≥3 distinct feels + melee fallback authored as data; swap (1/2/3, Q/E), ammo in HUD, charge-on-hold supported; unarmed slot 0 exists; unlocks/ammo serialized. |
| **S8 enemy combat** | Guard has hp/hurt, a fire mode emitting enemy Projectiles, and a 0.25s color-flash telegraph before any hitbox goes live; all values via DIFFICULTY. |
| **S9 SequenceDirector** | Authors a chase or gauntlet purely as data `[(t,fn)]` + per-frame spawners; win/lose checks; **lose → load auto-slot at sequence entry** (cheap retry, no zone loss). |
| **S10 juice** | screenshake (decaying cam offset), hitstop (skip sim, keep draw, ~40–60ms), white hit-flash, dash ghosts, telegraph rect/cone that fills over wind-up. All hookable in one call each. |
| **S11 Tax-Wagon** | Two phases playable start→finish; all three moral resolutions reachable; fails route to auto-reload; **passes the fun bar in playtest with the dash feeling crunchy.** |
| **S12 inventory** | I/TAB grid (pauses world), quick-heal H, key-items tab with contextual Z-use, enemy/crate drops via `money_mult`/`ammo_drop_mult`; items/key_items/money serialized. |
| **S13 scene format** | Act 1 fully authored in the data format (§5); no hardcoded Act-1 triggers remain in game.py beyond the dispatcher. |
| **S14 shop** | Buy/sell ListMenu shows price/money/one rumor; stock & prices flex by dominant path; buy/sell SFX. |
| **S15 stealth** | Guards have `alert_level` 0–3, `last_seen` search-then-sweep, hearing pings on sprint/dash/gunfire, shadow/lit tile detection mults, behind-guard Z takedown branching knockout(Mercy)/kill(Cruelty); KO'd/dead guard ids serialized per zone. |
| **S16 morality glue** | statuses tick + draw; intimidate prompt (cruel + low-HP target → flee), spare prompt (broken target → end/recruit), relationship ints serialized and read by dialogue/shop/endings; **spare/recruit gives an immediate legible reward, not only deferred (§7 incentive fix).** |
| **S17 puzzle primitives** | Pushable (1-tile, blocked-check, no softlock layouts), Lever↔gate live toggle, pressure plate, Mirror 90° reflect via raycast, ordered-input pattern list — each with a stable string id that round-trips through the save flags set. |
| **S18 system-boss kit** | Ankam + Warden authored as boss-phase data (§5) composing panels/waves/cones; three resolutions each; clean-solve weakens next phase. |
| **S19 escort** | Follower lerps along a fixed waypoint path, pauses when player stops, never pathfinds; spotted = fail → auto-reload; no panic-AI. |
| **S20–S22 stretch** | Each ships only after its act's predecessors clear playtest; Simon-says is dt-safe; lying-doors never softlock and survive save/load mid-loop; Sufflok phases + endings all reachable. |

## 6. Minimum Shippable vs Stretch

### MINIMUM SHIPPABLE GAME (the contract — must be complete & releasable)
- **Acts 1–3 to ship quality** + a **truncated Act 6 finale** delivering the portal reveal and **one distinct ending per moral path** (Mercy / Survival / Cruelty).
- Systems **S0–S19** (Tiers 0–5).
- One chase boss (Tax-Wagon), one puzzle boss (Ankam), one system boss (Warden).
- Core verbs: HP/death/save-reload, dash i-frames, projectile combat + ≥3 weapons, item/shop economy, upgraded stealth + takedowns, intimidate/spare/recruit, sokoban + lever + mirror + pattern-memory puzzles, scripted escort.
- **Each act validated in playtest before the next is authored.** S11 (Tax-Wagon) is the go/no-go gate for the whole pivot.

### STRETCH (only after the minimum lands)
- **Act 4** (Theater, Simon-says, Ringmaster/Prima) — S20.
- **Act 5** (Underground, lying-doors, Riddle-King) — S21.
- **Full Act 6** (ally-assist synthesis, multi-phase Sufflok, all 5 endings incl. early-exit & neutral) — S22.
- Echo-rooms, second weapon upgrades, NG+ carry-forward, codex lore cards.

## 7. Risks & Mitigations

| # | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Scope abandonment** (10–20× codebase; dies in Act 3). | Vertical slice first (Acts 1 + Tax-Wagon); ship the 3-act spine as a *complete* game; every act independently releasable; Tiers gated on playtest. |
| 2 | **Existing `save.py` ignored / spec disconnect.** | **Reuse `jun_world/save.py` SaveManager and its slot scheme (auto/1/2/3, `slot_%s.json`). Do not write a parallel `saves.py`.** Read code before specing the next system. |
| 3 | **Onboarding cliff** — 6 literacies stacked on a "move & talk" tutorial. | Verbs introduced one at a time; **isolated no-stakes dash corridor before the boss**; HP/save/Sling each get a dedicated safe beat. |
| 4 | **Mercy is punished → trains cruelty.** | **Immediate legible reward for non-lethal:** spared/recruited enemies drop *more* resources than killed; knockouts grant a brief safe window; unarmed-near-NPC unlocks instant shop discount/free item. Cruelty stays fast but spends something felt *now* (allies refuse trade, prices spike). Validate by watching whether an optimizer drifts mercy or cruelty. |
| 5 | **No concrete balance numbers** → swings trivial↔rage. | Ship `S.DIFFICULTY` (HAUNTED default) with explicit targets: **unaware player dies in 3 hits; careful player clears boss phase-1 in ~4 dodge windows; i-frame window 160ms; dash cd 550ms; basic guard 3 sling-hits; heal restores ~1/3 bar; ammo such that player is usually one bad fight from trouble, never softlocked broke.** Tune the slice to these before authoring more. |
| 6 | **Highest-bug features eat weeks.** | De-risk for v1: escort = scripted follow (no panic-AI); rhythm → Simon-says memory (dt-safe); non-Euclidean → lying-doors with one fixed honest cue + guaranteed exit after N loops; **cut enemy-AoE-hurts-neutrals as a moral consequence** (neutrals are obstacles only). |
| 7 | **Telegraphs-that-lie breaks the fairness contract.** | Honest cue taught in a zero-stakes room, named once by the Riddle Creature, *before* any damage; threat stays hard, rule stays fair. |
| 8 | **Lying-doors softlock / save-load corruption.** | Loop logic must always expose a discoverable exit, never trap with zero info, and serialize loop-counter + puzzle state; add the N-loop fallback exit; test save/load mid-loop explicitly. |
| 9 | **Stealth vs guns incoherence.** | Each zone hard-commits: **stealth-primary** (Prison, castle infiltration — gun is a last-resort escalation to fight/flee) vs **combat-primary** (arenas, bosses — stealth irrelevant). Transition is diegetic (alarm = you're in the gun game now). Not co-equal everywhere. |
| 10 | **Projectiles render under walls.** | Mandate y-sorted `draw_list` insertion for all new sprites (documented in §5). |

## Act-by-Act Narrative

### Act 1 — The Ruined Gate (Refined)  
*Zone: Ruined Villages*  ·  *~? min*

The existing portal-fall intro, ruined-village hub, depot stealth heist, and Mr. Ankam confrontation are kept but retuned to be the game's tutorial-with-teeth. The 'music note checkpoint' is REMOVED entirely; in its place the village well becomes a SAVE SHRINE (open save-slot menu, autosave on zone entry). Death and capture now matter: a third depot capture is a real GAME OVER -> load-from-save, not a free respawn. A new HEALTH (3 hearts) and STAMINA bar arrives via Rook, who hands you a worn SLING (your first ranged weapon: short-range stun pebble that briefly staggers a guard, replacing the old infinite 'F=noise' with a limited, aimed, cooldown-gated tool). The finale is expanded: after the Ankam choice, the village is RAIDED and you must survive a timed escape (your first run-away sequence) out the collapsing east road, teaching dashing past sightlines under pressure.

**New mechanics:** Save-slot system (3 slots) at save shrines + autosave on zone entry; removal of note-checkpoint respawn, Game Over screen -> load save (death/3rd-capture is punishing, not free), Health (hearts) and stamina resource introduced, The Sling: aimed short-range stun projectile with cooldown + limited pebbles (item resource), Dash with brief i-frames bound to stamina (taught in the escape), Timed run-away/escape sequence under collapsing terrain + guard sightlines

**Key characters:** Player, Rook, Mr. Ankam, Nervous Villager, Sufflok (notices/voice only)

**Beats:**
- Portal-fall intro plays unchanged; player wakes on the cracked road, learns move/interact.
- Village hub: wandering villagers, readable Sufflok notices, the optional Nervous Villager moral beat (mercy/survival/cruelty hint for the medicine stash).
- The well is revealed as the first SAVE SHRINE; a tooltip teaches save slots and that autosave fires on entering any new zone.
- Meeting Rook + tone choice; Rook now also gives you the Sling and explains hearts/stamina, framing survival as scarce.
- Depot heist retuned harder: tighter guard loops, faster detection, the Sling can stun-stagger one guard briefly to slip a sightline; getting caught burns toward a real Game Over.
- Return the three crates to Rook; reaction dialogue varies by dominant moral lean.
- Mr. Ankam marches in with guards; the 3-way confrontation choice (mercy/survival/cruelty) resolves as before but now SETS a persistent 'ankam_disposition' flag used across all later acts.
- NEW: A castle horn sounds — the raid. Guards flood the village. A timed escape begins; you must dash through gaps in vision cones and over rubble before the east road fully collapses.
- Cruel players can spend the escape INTIMIDATING a guard into fleeing (opening a path); merciful players can pull a trapped villager free (costs time, gains trust); survival players find the fastest clean route.
- Clear the road; an ending-card sequence reflects the dominant path AND seeds the portal mystery ('the sky used to be a door').
- Autosave locks in your Act One identity and items as you stagger toward the castle kingdom.

**Boss:** No true boss — instead a 'pursuit miniboss': a single elite Captain Guard with a wider, faster-sweeping vision cone leads the raid escape. He cannot be killed here, only outrun/stunned/intimidated, establishing that fleeing and resource use are valid victory states.

**Puzzle:** Light environmental gate: during the escape, a collapsed cart blocks the road; the player must shove a barrel (single-tile push, the engine's first sokoban primitive) onto a pressure plank to drop a fence, or stun the guard standing on it — one mechanical action, two solutions by play style.

**Moral branching:** Mercy: saving the trapped villager later yields a Former Castle Servant contact in Act Three. Survival: cleanest escape time is logged and unlocks a shopkeeper discount. Cruelty: intimidating the guard makes him reappear in Act Two as a broken, fearful informant who calls you 'the other one like him.' Ankam's disposition (spared-with-pity / out-maneuvered / terrified) persists into every act.

### Act 2 — The Theatrical Crown  
*Zone: Twisted Castle Kingdom (royal, unfair, theatrical)*  ·  *~? min*

Past the gate lies Sufflok's outer kingdom: a gaudy, gold-and-rot court town run on absurd 'royal law.' This act escalates into open PROJECTILE COMBAT. The Sling is upgraded by the WEIRD SHOPKEEPER (introduced here) into a proper ranged weapon, and you gain an ITEM INVENTORY (heal items, throwables, keys). Enemies — Court Pikemen and Decree-Heralds — now actively shoot back in arena skirmishes, teaching dash-dodging through bullet patterns. The zone is gated by THEATRICAL TRIALS: rigged contests where the 'rules' are designed for you to lose, and the real puzzle is breaking or exploiting them.

**New mechanics:** Full projectile combat: enemies fire telegraphed shots; player dashes/strafes with i-frames, Weapon inventory + upgrades (Sling -> Whipcord Sling: charged shot, ricochet), Item inventory UI (consumables: bandage=heal, smokebead=brief invisibility, lockpick, throwable decoys), Resource scarcity economy: a real currency ('chits') bought/sold at the Weird Shopkeeper, Arena skirmishes with multiple simultaneous shooters + cover positioning, Rigged-rules trial puzzles (exploit the letter of an unfair law to win)

**Key characters:** Player, Rook, Weird Shopkeeper, Mr. Ankam, Court Herald (named minor antagonist)

**Beats:**
- Enter the court town: theatrical pageantry over visible cruelty — a 'Festival of Gratitude' nobody is grateful for.
- Meet the WEIRD SHOPKEEPER in a crooked stall: sells items, rumors, and half-truths; upgrades the Sling; first real economy and inventory tutorial.
- First arena skirmish: Court Pikemen open fire; learn to dash through telegraphed volleys and use pillars as cover.
- Rook reconnects, now hunted by name; his recklessness pushes a risky plan to disrupt the Festival; relationship tone shifts by your Act One path.
- Trial of the Crooked Court: a rigged 'fair contest' (a race / a 'voluntary' tribute weigh-in) whose rules guarantee your loss — solve by exploiting the exact wording (e.g., the law never said the tribute had to be YOURS).
- Mr. Ankam reappears in an official capacity; depending on his Act One disposition he either nervously tips you off, stiffly obstructs you, or visibly flinches from you — his arc as informant/tool/saboteur begins branching here.
- A captured villager (or the Act One villager you saved) is to be 'awarded' to Sufflok on stage; the heist becomes a rescue.
- Shopkeeper reveals a half-truth about the portal: 'doors that open both ways were outlawed for a reason' — first real portal lore beyond rumor.
- Escalating skirmish through the festival backstage; multiple shooters + a sokoban prop-cart puzzle to reach the stage.
- Boss: THE HERALD'S GRAND FINALE (see boss concept).
- Resolution: the Festival collapses into farce or terror depending on how you won; the kingdom's fear of you (cruelty) or hope in you (mercy) is now visibly tracked by NPC reactions.
- Exit toward the Prison Zones as Sufflok, watching from afar, takes personal interest for the first time.

**Boss:** THE COURT HERALD (first true boss): a pompous, amplified announcer who fights theatrically — he never attacks directly but CONDUCTS waves of Pikemen and stage hazards (falling sandbags, spotlight 'kill zones' that sweep the arena, confetti-cannon bullet spreads). Three phases escalate the spotlight patterns and shooter count. Mercy path: you can win by exposing his script live (a dialogue/puzzle takedown) so the crowd laughs and he flees, no killing. Survival: disable the stage rigging to neutralize him efficiently. Cruelty: out-perform him so totally the crowd turns on HIM, and he is dragged off — your first taste of weaponizing the crowd's fear.

**Puzzle:** The Rigged Decree: an unfair law posted in three scorched fragments around the festival; reading all three reveals a loophole. Combined with a spotlight-timing dodge puzzle (cross the stage only while the sweeping light is elsewhere) and a barrel/prop push to redirect a confetti cannon as a weapon against the boss rigging.

**Moral branching:** Mercy unlocks recruiting a defecting Herald's-apprentice who later helps in the prison. Cruelty makes court NPCs flee on sight and unlocks 'intimidate' as a combat option that makes weaker shooters surrender. Survival keeps the shopkeeper's best prices and the cleanest reputation. The villager-rescue outcome (saved/lost) and Ankam's reaction here both persist. Rook grows prouder of a merciful you, warier of a cruel you (he starts withholding plans if you scare him).

### Act 3 — The Cold Ledger  
*Zone: Prison Zones (cold, bureaucratic)*  ·  *~? min*

A grey machine of cells, checkpoints, and paperwork where cruelty is administrative, not theatrical. This act is the STEALTH-and-SYSTEMS peak: keycard tiers, patrol schedules, camera/searchlight cones, and a full ESCORT mechanic — you must break out prisoners and guide fragile, panicking NPCs past detection. The FORMER CASTLE SERVANT joins as a guide who knows the prison's customs and confirms a crucial truth: Mr. Ankam's fear of Sufflok is genuine, reframing him as a victim. Resource scarcity bites hardest here (no shop; you live on what you carry).

**New mechanics:** Keycard / clearance-tier locked doors (inventory keys gate progression), Searchlight + static camera vision cones (environmental detectors, not just guards), ESCORT/defend mechanic: lead fragile prisoners who panic, freeze, or bolt if spotted, Alarm-state escalation: lockdowns seal doors and spawn responders on a timer, Lever/switch + circuit puzzles (reroute power to kill searchlights or open cellblocks), Hard resource scarcity (no merchant; manage health/ammo across a long level)

**Key characters:** Player, Former Castle Servant, Rook, Mr. Ankam, Warden (bureaucratic antagonist)

**Beats:**
- Arrival: the prison processes people like cargo; tone shifts from theatrical to clinical dread.
- Meet the FORMER CASTLE SERVANT, imprisoned for 'knowing too much'; she becomes your guide to castle customs and clearance tiers.
- Tutorial of systems: keycards, camera cones, and the lever-circuit that powers searchlights.
- First escort: free a terrified prisoner and walk them to an exit — they freeze in light, bolt at alarms, teaching patience and route control.
- The Servant confirms Mr. Ankam's terror of Sufflok is REAL — he keeps the ledger because his own name is in it; this recontextualizes every prior Ankam scene.
- Mr. Ankam appears at his arc's pivot: depending on disposition he secretly leaves a cell unlocked (informant), enforces the lockdown against you (tool), or — if terrified of you — accidentally triggers a chaos that helps you (accidental saboteur).
- Deep-prison circuit puzzle: reroute power across a grid of levers to black out a searchlight corridor and open the high-security block.
- Mass-cellblock decision: a loud break frees everyone but triggers a hunting lockdown; a quiet break saves a few cleanly — a survival-vs-mercy structural choice.
- Defend sequence: hold a chokepoint while the Servant unseals the final gate, dodging responder volleys with limited ammo.
- Boss: THE WARDEN (see boss concept).
- Aftermath: freed prisoners' fate depends on your loud/quiet choice and combat conduct; the Servant pledges to get you castle clearance for the finale.
- A recovered ledger page reveals Sufflok's true fear — outsiders who came through doors BEFORE you — pushing the mystery toward the Nightmare Underground.

**Boss:** THE WARDEN: a cold administrator who fights by SYSTEM, not by hand. She never enters your range; she operates the prison against you — sealing arena sections with shutters, activating searchlight 'kill cones' you must dodge, and dispatching numbered responder squads in scripted waves. You defeat her by destroying her four control panels (each guarded, each requiring a dash through a bullet/searchlight gauntlet) rather than damaging her directly. Mercy: spare her at the end and she quietly resigns her post, becoming a silent ally who 'loses' your file. Survival: disable the panels and lock her in her own control room. Cruelty: turn her own lockdown on her — the system she worshiped erases her, and she dies begging, which unsettles even Rook.

**Puzzle:** The Power Ledger: a multi-lever circuit puzzle where each lever toggles two corridors of searchlights (a lights-out / XOR-style puzzle); the player must find the lever sequence that darkens a path while keeping a needed door powered. Layered with keycard-tier gating and an escort who can't cross lit tiles.

**Moral branching:** Loud vs quiet cellblock break sets how many allies appear at the finale. Sparing/erasing the Warden gates a late ally and a Rook trust shift. The Former Castle Servant's loyalty (and whether she shares the full ledger truth) depends on whether you escorted prisoners gently or used them as cover. Ankam's accidental-saboteur path can only trigger if he was left genuinely terrified of you in Acts One/Two — explicitly rewarding a consistent cruel run with a darkly comic, plot-critical accident.

### Act 4 — Applause, On Pain Of  
*Zone: Forced Theater / Performance Areas (forced loyalty shows)*  ·  *~? min*

A surreal performance complex where the regime stages 'spontaneous' loyalty shows — funny on the surface, humiliating and coercive underneath. The signature mechanic is the RHYTHM/TIMING GAUNTLET: you must PERFORM on cue (hit timed inputs to clap, bow, march, recite) to blend in, while secretly sabotaging the show. Failing a cue draws guard attention (a detection layer fused with timing). This act is the emotional hinge: it's where the regime's psychology — fear performed as love — is laid bare, and where Mr. Ankam's arc reaches its peak, because forced theater is HIS domain and his deepest shame.

**New mechanics:** Rhythm/timing gauntlet: hit on-beat inputs to 'perform' loyalty; misses raise suspicion, Performance-as-stealth: blend into choreographed crowds, breaking from script only in blind windows, Disguise system: wear a performer costume (inventory) to pass checkpoints until you act wrong, Pattern-memory puzzles (repeat the regime's anthem/gesture sequence back correctly), Dual-objective tension: perform convincingly AND sabotage props/wiring between cues, Crowd-sentiment meter you can sway (mercy = inspire, cruelty = terrify the audience)

**Key characters:** Player, Mr. Ankam, Rook, Former Castle Servant, Riddle Creature (first cameo, in the rafters)

**Beats:**
- Enter the Theater District: relentless forced cheer, performers with exhausted eyes, applause signs that threaten.
- Disguise as a performer; learn the timing-gauntlet — clap/bow/march on cue to pass, or draw suspicion.
- First sabotage-under-performance: rewire a prop spotlight between cues without missing a beat.
- Mr. Ankam is revealed as the reluctant DIRECTOR of the loyalty shows — terrified, humiliated, hating himself; his arc peaks as he must choose whether to expose you.
- If Ankam is your informant/ally: he feeds you the cue-sheet, making the gauntlet survivable, and quietly confesses why he stays. If he's the tool: he runs the show against you. If accidental-saboteur: his nervous fumbling becomes your cover.
- The Riddle Creature appears briefly in the rafters, speaking one riddle that foreshadows the Underground and the portal's nature — first real contact.
- Pattern-memory set piece: recite/perform the regime anthem-gesture sequence back perfectly to open the grand stage.
- Crowd-sentiment climax: you get the stage mic; sway the audience — inspire them toward quiet hope (mercy), give them a clean exit (survival), or terrify them into turning on their handlers (cruelty).
- Sabotage cascades; the forced show breaks into genuine, dangerous chaos.
- Boss: THE MASKED PRIMA (see boss concept).
- Mr. Ankam's fate resolves: he can defect and flee with you, be left behind broken, or — on a cruel run — be so frightened he becomes Sufflok's informant against you for the finale, a tragic betrayal born of terror.
- Exit: a trapdoor in the stage drops into the Nightmare Underground, mid-portal-mystery.

**Boss:** THE MASKED PRIMA: the regime's 'perfect performer,' a multi-phase rhythm boss. Her attacks are CHOREOGRAPHY — bullet patterns that fire on a musical beat, and 'call-and-response' phases where she performs a gesture sequence you must dodge in the same rhythm. Phase shifts change the tempo. Survival is reading the beat. Mercy: match her perfectly and 'duet' rather than fight, revealing she's a coerced prisoner behind the mask — you free her. Survival: break her props mid-routine to desync her patterns and end it efficiently. Cruelty: upstage her so brutally the crowd's adoration flips to her, breaking her — she collapses, and the realization that you used the regime's own cruelty trick lands hard, especially on Rook.

**Puzzle:** The Cue-Sheet: a memory/timing puzzle layered over stealth — you watch a choreographed sequence (clap-clap-bow-march), then must reproduce it on cue while a guard's suspicion meter ticks on every miss. Combined with between-cue sabotage windows (cut three prop wires, each only reachable during a specific blind beat) to break the show.

**Moral branching:** Mr. Ankam's ENTIRE fate resolves here and changes the finale: ally-defector (he opens a castle door for you), broken-bystander (neutral), or terrified-informant (he warns Sufflok, adding a finale obstacle) — keyed to his accumulated disposition across Acts 1-4. The crowd-sentiment outcome determines whether Theater-district NPCs aid your finale assault, flee, or riot. Mercy 'duet' with the Prima recruits her; cruelty 'upstaging' her costs you Rook trust and tints the cruel ending darker.

### Act 5 — The Door That Lies  
*Zone: Nightmare Underground (surreal, looping rooms, doors that lie)*  ·  *~? min*

The reality-bending heart of the game and the home of the PORTAL MYSTERY's revelation. Rooms loop, doors lead where their labels lie, geometry shifts, and a creeping DREAD meter replaces simple detection — staying too long in a 'wrong' room warps the space and spawns nightmares. The signature mechanics are NON-EUCLIDEAN NAVIGATION PUZZLES and RIDDLE-GATED doors run by the RIDDLE CREATURE, who is fully introduced as the mystery's keeper. Here the player learns WHY the portal opened, why THEY were chosen, and what Sufflok truly fears: that he, too, is an outsider who came through a door and never went home.

**New mechanics:** Looping/shifting room navigation (doors that relabel; correct path found by logic, not brute force), Dread meter: a psychological timer — lingering warps rooms and births nightmare enemies, Riddle-gated doors: answer the Riddle Creature's riddles (dialogue puzzles) to pass, Light/shadow mechanic: a carried lantern reveals true doors vs lying ones, Nightmare combat: surreal enemies with unfair-feeling but learnable patterns, Memory/echo puzzles: rooms replay your OWN past choices as obstacles or keys

**Key characters:** Player, Riddle Creature, Rook, Sufflok (revelation, indirect), echoes of past characters

**Beats:**
- Fall into the Underground: physics feel wrong; a corridor loops back on itself until you notice the lie.
- The RIDDLE CREATURE is fully introduced — ancient, sad, funny, speaking only in riddles; it guards the path and the truth.
- Lantern mechanic: true doors glow under light; lying doors look identical in the dark — learn to test before trusting.
- First riddle-gate: solve to pass; the riddle's answer encodes a piece of portal lore.
- Dread set piece: a room that warps the longer you stay; solve its spatial puzzle before nightmares overwhelm you.
- Echo rooms: the dungeon replays YOUR earlier moral choices as physical obstacles — a cruel player faces the people they frightened; a merciful player is helped by those they saved (mechanically: past choices become keys or walls).
- Revelation core: the Riddle Creature reveals Sufflok was once an outsider like you, came through a door, and built his tyranny out of the terror of never getting home — 'fear can become a crown.'
- You learn the portal opened FOR you the way it once opened for him; going home and toppling him are tangled together.
- A surreal mirror-confrontation: a nightmare version of YOU shaped by your dominant moral path (mercy-self, survival-self, cruelty-self) — fight or reconcile.
- Boss: THE LOOPING WARDEN OF DOORS (see boss concept).
- Choice fulcrum: the Riddle Creature offers a way home RIGHT NOW (abandon the world) or the way to Sufflok (finish it) — a real branch that flavors the ending.
- Emerge at the foot of Sufflok's true castle, carrying the truth as a weapon.

**Boss:** THE LOOPING WARDEN OF DOORS (a.k.a. The Unanswered): a boss made of doors and echoes that fights across a looping arena. It teleports between false doors (only the lantern-lit true door is the real one you can strike), and attacks with mirrored versions of mechanics from earlier acts (a stealth cone, a rhythm volley, a rigged decree) — a 'greatest hits' test. Its final phase wears YOUR nightmare-self's face. Mercy: answer its last riddle instead of fighting — it dissolves gratefully, having waited eons for a correct answer. Survival: methodically track the true door each cycle and end it. Cruelty: it feeds on fear and grows stronger if you've been cruel, making your own path the hardest fight — a pointed mechanical consequence.

**Puzzle:** The Lying Doors: a navigation logic puzzle where door labels lie according to a discoverable rule (e.g., every label is the opposite of the truth, or labels rotate each loop). The lantern reveals true exits but drains; the Riddle Creature's riddles give the rule. Topped with an echo-puzzle where your own past saved/frightened characters appear as bridges or barricades, literally making your morality the map.

**Moral branching:** The mirror-self fight differs entirely by dominant path (reconcile vs subdue vs overpower). The 'go home now vs finish it' choice is a major branch: leaving early triggers an early/partial ending; staying carries the truth to the finale. Whether echoes help or hinder is a direct, legible payoff of every prior choice. Cruelty literally makes this act harder, embodying the theme 'cruelty works fast but costs you.'

### Act 6 — The Outsider's Crown  
*Zone: Sufflok's Castle (finale)*  ·  *~? min*

The convergence. Every system, ally, item, and moral debt is called in for the assault on Sufflok's castle and the confrontation with the tyrant himself. Allies recruited (Servant, Shopkeeper, Prima, defecting Herald-apprentice, freed prisoners, a defected-or-not Mr. Ankam) appear or are absent based on your record. Sufflok is revealed as a fellow outsider who weaponized his fear into structured cruelty — and he becomes obsessed with a CRUEL player as a worthy heir/rival, or unsettled by a MERCIFUL one who proves fear didn't have to become this. The multi-phase final boss tests every mechanic; the portal mystery pays off as the literal stakes: control of the door home.

**New mechanics:** Full mechanics synthesis: stealth, projectile combat, dash-dodge gauntlets, puzzles, escort, rhythm — interleaved, Ally-assist system: recruited characters provide combat support, opened doors, or revives based on your record, Multi-phase final boss with branching scripts per dominant moral path, Portal-as-arena mechanic: the unstable door alters gravity/teleports combatants in the last phase, Branching finale routes (assault / infiltrate / parley) keyed to morality + allies, Save-and-point-of-no-return gating with a final autosave

**Key characters:** Player, Sufflok, Rook, Mr. Ankam, Former Castle Servant, Weird Shopkeeper, Riddle Creature

**Beats:**
- Approach the black castle; allies you earned rally (or their absence is felt) — the game tallies your run made visible.
- Choose an approach: loud assault (combat-forward), infiltration (stealth/disguise callback), or parley attempt (mercy/dialogue) — gated by morality and allies.
- Gauntlet ascent: each floor reprises a prior act's signature mechanic as a final exam (a stealth cone hall, a rhythm corridor, a rigged-decree gate, a lying-door room).
- Mr. Ankam's final beat: defector-ally throws the throne-room doors for you; tool stands in your way one last time, terrified; informant has betrayed your route, forcing a harder path — his tragedy lands either as redemption or as fear winning.
- Rook's culmination: proud second to a merciful you, a hardened partner to a survivalist, or a frightened near-enemy to a cruel you who may try to stop you.
- Throne confrontation: Sufflok reveals he is an outsider who came through a door long ago; his cruelty is fear given STRUCTURE; he offers the cruel player partnership, fears the merciful one's example, respects the survivor's pragmatism.
- Boss: SUFFLOK (see boss concept) — multi-phase, mechanically synthesizing the whole game.
- Mid-fight, the portal home destabilizes into the arena itself; you fight over literal control of the door.
- The truth-as-weapon: revelations from the Underground can be used in dialogue to crack Sufflok's certainty (mercy/parley route can partially or fully defuse him).
- Climax resolves per route: spare/redeem, out-survive/seal away, or destroy/replace him.
- The portal opens; the final choice — go home, stay to rebuild, or take the crown — crystallizes your identity.
- Cut to the keyed ending (see endings).

**Boss:** SUFFLOK (final boss, multi-phase, branching): Phase 1 — the Tyrant: structured, 'fair' bullet patterns and decree-hazards reflecting his belief in control. Phase 2 — the Outsider: the mask slips; patterns grow frightened, erratic, mixing every prior boss's signature (Herald spotlights, Warden searchlights, Prima rhythm volleys, looping doors) as his fear takes over. Phase 3 — at the Door: the unstable portal warps the arena (gravity flips, teleports) and he fights as a cornered, terrified person who became a god of cruelty. MERCY route: using Underground truths, you can reach the scared outsider inside him and end the fight without killing — he lets the door open. SURVIVAL route: outlast and seal him on the wrong side of the door as it closes — pragmatic, bittersweet. CRUELTY route: he's THRILLED by you, fights to make you his heir; defeating him means becoming the thing he was — the hardest, darkest fight, where the crown is the prize and the cost.

**Puzzle:** The Final Door: a synthesis puzzle in the last phase — stabilize the portal by solving a lying-door/lever/rhythm hybrid (callbacks to Acts 3-5) WHILE dodging Sufflok, optionally aided by allies who hold panels open. On the parley route the 'puzzle' becomes a dialogue lattice: assemble the truths gathered all game (Ankam's fear is real, Sufflok came through a door, fear became a crown) in the right order to disarm him.

**Moral branching:** This is the total payoff: which allies appear, which approach routes unlock, whether Sufflok can be parleyed, and which of the multiple endings triggers are ALL determined by the dominant moral path plus key persistent flags (Ankam's fate, Rook's trust, Servant/Prima/Shopkeeper recruitment, the loud/quiet prison choice, the 'go home now' refusal in Act Five). Rook may stand beside you or against you. The crown is offered only to the cruel; mercy is offered the chance to prove fear need not become tyranny.

### Endings
- **The Door Home (Mercy / Spare-Sufflok)** — _Dominant MERCY, reached the parley route, used the Underground truths to defuse Sufflok, and kept Rook's trust (saved key NPCs: Act One villager, prisoners freed gently, Prima freed, Ankam defected or spared)._ — You were scared the whole way and you helped anyway. Sufflok, shown that fear did not HAVE to become a crown, lets the portal open rather than rule through it. The kingdom is left not conquered but un-terrified — fragile, free, choosing for itself. You step toward the door home, but it's now a door that opens both ways: you can return, and you can come back. Rook sees you off as a friend who proved active mercy is stronger than fear. Mr. Ankam (if defected) is left running a theater that finally performs nothing but the truth. The warmest ending; the world keeps its hope and so do you.
- **Still Breathing (Survival / Seal-the-Door)** — _Dominant SURVIVAL, defeated Sufflok pragmatically and sealed him on the wrong side as the portal closed; cleanest-route and resource-disciplined record; Rook respects you as a partner._ — You moved smart, spent nothing you didn't have to, and people are still breathing because of it. Sufflok is sealed away rather than redeemed or made a martyr — the structured cruelty simply switched off, like a machine unplugged. The world is wary, scarred, and standing. You get home, but the door seals behind you for good; you'll never know if it held. Bittersweet and clean: survival was morally messy, no one sang your name, but the ledger finally has fewer marks in it. Rook gives you a soldier's nod, the highest praise he has.
- **The New Crown (Cruelty / Become Sufflok)** — _Dominant CRUELTY, defeated Sufflok on the heir/rival route after he embraced you, terrified Ankam into betrayal, lost most of Rook's trust, and intimidated rather than spared throughout._ — You got results. People obeyed. And at the end, the tyrant looked at you the way the village once looked at HIM — with recognition. You take the crown. The door home is right there, and you do not walk through it, because here you are something; there you were no one. Cruelty worked fast, exactly as warned — and it cost you everyone who mattered. Rook flees or falls. The portal hums shut on a world that simply changed which outsider it feared. The darkest ending: you went home to a throne instead of a home.
- **The Outsider Who Left (Early Exit / Refused the World)** — _In Act Five, accepted the Riddle Creature's offer to go home immediately, OR reached the finale with no recruited allies and chose 'go home' at the final door without confronting Sufflok's truth._ — You chose yourself. The door home opens early and you take it, leaving Sufflok's world to its own long night. It's not cowardice exactly — you were never asked to save a world that wasn't yours — but the ending lingers on what you left behind: Rook's unfinished fight, a village still waiting, a tyrant still seated. You make it home. You're safe. And on quiet nights the torn-song sound flickers at the edge of your hearing, and you wonder what the shape of that choice says about you. An ambiguous, quietly haunting close that respects the player's right to simply leave.
- **The Empty Throne (Neutral / No Dominant Path)** — _No single moral path dominant at the finale (balanced mercy/survival/cruelty) and Sufflok defeated without parley or heir routes — the 'undefined identity' outcome._ — You never let yourself become any one thing. You spared and you frightened and you calculated, and in the end Sufflok falls but nothing replaces him — the throne sits empty because the person who toppled it never decided who they were. The world is free of him and unsure of you. You walk to the door home as a question mark, neither remembered as savior nor feared as tyrant. The most thematically pointed ending: fear became MANY things in you and settled into none, and the world is left to make of that what it will. Quietly unresolved, by design.

## Systems

### Save Files (slots, load, autosave, game-over)
- **Purpose:** Replace the removed music-note respawn with real persistence. On death the player no longer teleports to a glowing note; instead a GAME OVER screen offers LOAD or QUIT, so death has weight and the world state is authoritative. Three manual slots plus one rolling autosave give the player control over how much progress they risk.
- **Controls:** Title screen gains a vertical menu (reuse a new ListMenu widget): NEW GAME / CONTINUE (most-recent save) / LOAD GAME / QUIT, navigated with W/S or Up/Down, confirmed with Z/Enter. In-play, Esc opens a PAUSE menu (new mode 'pause') with RESUME / SAVE / LOAD / QUIT TO TITLE. SAVE opens a 3-slot list showing zone name, playtime, dominant path, and timestamp; pick a slot with Up/Down + Z to overwrite (confirm prompt if occupied). Manual save is BLOCKED during combat/cutscene/chase (HUD shows 'Can't save now'). On death: GAME OVER mode with LOAD (jump to slot list) or QUIT.
- **Design:** Add a new module saves.py. A snapshot is one JSON dict written with the stdlib `json` module to a per-user dir resolved without external deps: `os.path.join(os.path.expanduser('~'), '.jun_world', 'saves')`, created with `os.makedirs(..., exist_ok=True)`. Files: slot0.json/slot1.json/slot2.json and auto.json, plus meta written inline (version int, timestamp via time.time(), playtime float, zone, dominant path, a short auto-generated 'chapter' label). Serialize ONLY plain run state — never pygame Surfaces or live entity objects. The serializable bundle: moral dict, sorted(flags) list, quest string, current scene name + player tile (x//TILE, y//TILE) + facing, inventory (item id->count), equipped weapon id, ammo dict, weapon unlock set, HP/maxHP, money, per-zone progress dicts (depot_taken, boss flags, switch/puzzle states keyed by stable string ids), and the moral/relationship counters (rook_respect, ankam_arc, etc.). load_game() reconstructs by calling a refactored `apply_save(data)` that sets attributes then calls load_scene(scene, computed_spawn) and overrides player.x/y to the saved tile. Autosave fires on every scene transition (_do_transition) and after major story beats, writing auto.json silently with a brief 'Autosaved' toast. - **Difficulty impact:** This is the backbone of the harder game: removing free checkpoints means mistakes cost real progress. Autosave-on-transition guarantees the player never loses more than one zone of work, keeping it punishing-but-fair. Manual saves let cautious players bank progress before a boss; reckless players who skip them feel the tension. Death routing through GAME OVER -> LOAD (not instant respawn) restores stakes the note mechanic erased.
- **Implementation:** Refactor `_reset_run` into `new_game_state()` (the NEW GAME path) and add `apply_save(data)`. Add modes 'pause','load_menu','save_menu','gameover' to the `self.mode` switch in update()/draw()/on_key(). Build a reusable `ListMenu` in ui.py (items with label+sublabel+enabled flag, draw with rounded_panel, same nav sound hooks as ChoiceBox). Stable puzzle/boss ids (e.g. 'castle_lever_A') must be strings so the flags set round-trips cleanly through JSON as a list. Tie-in to assets.Audio: add 'menu_open' and 'gameover' synth one-shots alongside existing 'save'.
- **Theme tie:** Permanence reinforces 'cruelty works too fast' and 'survival is morally messy' — a cruel snap decision is now saved into your record and cannot be quietly undone by a respawn. The autosave chapter labels narrate your drift between Mercy/Survival/Cruelty so the save list itself reads like a moral ledger, echoing Mr. Ankam's dreaded 'ledger' line.

### Difficulty Overhaul (harder but fair)
- **Purpose:** Make the world tense and punishing without being cheap. Convert the current forgiving stealth-only loop into a survival experience where resources, positioning, and read-the-pattern skill all matter, and death is a real setback (no free note respawn).
- **Controls:** No new bindings; difficulty is expressed through existing movement/dodge/combat/stealth controls being meaningful. A one-time DIFFICULTY pick on NEW GAME (STORY / HAUNTED / CRUEL WORLD) via the ListMenu sets global multipliers; default is HAUNTED (the intended hard baseline). Optional in-pause toggle to drop difficulty (one-way, with a small flag so a 'no-mercy-mode' achievement-style ending tag stays honest).
- **Design:** Centralize tunables in settings.py as a DIFFICULTY dict of multipliers: enemy_damage, enemy_hp, enemy_speed, vision_range_mult, detect_rate_mult, ammo_drop_mult, heal_strength_mult, money_mult, boss_phase_hp. Concrete hardening levers: (1) Guards in stealth now have a faster DETECT_RATE and shorter detection decay, plus a brief 'last known position' search instead of instantly forgetting. (2) Getting spotted no longer means a soft chase-and-respawn — guards deal contact damage to the new HP system, and an alarm summons reinforcements from gate spawns on a timer. (3) Resource scarcity: ammo and healing items are limited and must be bought/found, so panic-spraying is punished. (4) Enemy variety scales per zone (basic guard -> shielded guard that must be flanked -> ranged crossbow guard -> elite). (5) Environmental hazards (spike floors in prison, collapsing tiles in nightmare) add a solid/damage tile type. Fairness rules: generous i-frames after every hit, telegraphed enemy attacks (wind-up flash before damage), no off-screen instant kills, and autosave-on-transition so a death costs at most one zone.
- **Difficulty impact:** This system IS the difficulty curve. Each multiplier is data, so the integrator can tune the whole game from one table. The combination of contact damage + limited ammo + reinforcement timers turns the depot-style stealth from 'undetectable forever' into a tense smash-and-grab where mistakes compound.
- **Implementation:** Add `S.DIFFICULTY` dict and read it everywhere a raw constant is used (e.g. `dmg = base * S.DIFFICULTY['enemy_damage']`). Add new tile props to world.py PROPS: 'x' spike (solid=False, damage-on-enter), '^' collapsing (becomes solid after a step delay), 'L' lava/void (damage + knockback). Hazard checks go in a new `_update_hazards(dt)` that samples the tile under player.feet() each frame and applies damage through the HP system's `hurt()` (respecting i-frames). Reinforcement timer: when alerted, start a countdown; on expiry, spawn 1-2 guards at the nearest gate transition and move_actor them toward last-known position. Store chosen difficulty in the save bundle. Keep telegraphs cheap: a 0.25s color-flash on the enemy sprite before its hitbox activates, drawn in the existing actor draw path.
- **Theme tie:** 'Cruelty works too fast' and 'survival is morally messy' become mechanical truths: the world is genuinely dangerous, so a player tempted toward cruelty (intimidation that makes enemies flee) gets a real, tested-under-pressure shortcut — and pays for it in how characters treat them. Scarcity makes every mercy (sparing an enemy who could have dropped loot) a measured sacrifice.

### Player Health, Damage & I-Frames
- **Purpose:** Give the player a concrete life resource so combat, hazards, and chases can threaten death — the prerequisite for every other punishing system. Replaces the old binary 'caught -> note respawn' with graded damage and real death.
- **Controls:** No direct input; HP is consumed by taking hits and restored by consumables (see Inventory). A heart/segment HP bar renders top-left. Brief screen-edge red flash + screenshake on hit gives unmistakable feedback. When HP hits 0 -> GAME OVER (Save Files system).
- **Design:** Add HP fields to Player: `hp`, `max_hp` (start ~6 'hearts', i.e. 60 with 10/heart for granularity), `inv_t` (invulnerability timer), `hurt_flash`. A `hurt(amount, source_pos=None)` method: if inv_t>0, ignore; else subtract amount*difficulty, set inv_t to ~0.9s, trigger knockback away from source_pos (a short velocity impulse resolved through the existing per-axis collision), set hurt_flash, play 'hurt' sound (already synthesized), add screenshake. During i-frames the sprite blinks (skip draw on alternating ~60ms windows). Death (hp<=0) sets a dying flag, plays 'caught'/new 'gameover' sound, fades to the GAME OVER mode. Healing clamps to max_hp. Max HP can grow via key-item upgrades (e.g. a 'Steady Heart' found in the nightmare zone, +1 heart).
- **Difficulty impact:** HP is the meter the whole difficulty table pushes against: enemy_damage and hazard damage are balanced so an unaware player dies in a few hits, while a careful one survives gauntlets. Generous i-frames + knockback keep it fair by preventing stun-locks and multi-hit-per-frame chip death.
- **Implementation:** Add hp/inv_t/hurt_flash to Player.__init__ in entities.py and an `hurt()` method there; decrement inv_t in a new per-frame `player.update(dt)` call from Game.update. Knockback: store `kbx,kby` velocity that decays over ~0.18s and is applied via the existing `_axis` collision so walls stop it. Screenshake: add `self.shake` to Game, offset the camera blit by `random.uniform(-shake,shake)` while shake>0 and decay it; reuse for hits, explosions, boss slams. HP bar draw: new `ui.draw_health(surf, fonts, hp, max_hp)` using heart glyphs drawn from primitives (filled/half/empty) in the existing palette (SUFFLOK_RED for fill, INK for empty). Serialize hp/max_hp in the save bundle. Wire the old `_caught()` path to instead call `player.hurt(big)` on grab so capture is lethal-ish but consistent with the HP model.
- **Theme tie:** The player being a scared, fragile outsider is now literal — you are squishy in a world built to crush you, reinforcing 'fear can become many things'. Surviving on low HP through a moral choice (sparing instead of killing) costs you safety, making mercy an active, risky verb rather than a free option.

### Dodge / Dash with I-Frames
- **Purpose:** Give the player a skill-expression movement tool to survive projectile gauntlets, boss patterns, and chases — the core 'feel-good' verb that makes hard fights fair and fun.
- **Controls:** Tap SPACE (or a dedicated key, e.g. K_LSHIFT repurposed in combat zones / K_k) to dash a fixed distance in the current movement direction (or last facing if standing still). Short i-frame window at the start of the dash lets you pass through projectiles and enemy contact. A cooldown (with a small UI pip that refills) prevents spam. Hold-to-sneak Shift remains for stealth zones; dash uses its own key so the two never conflict.
- **Design:** Add dash state to Player: `dash_t`, `dash_cd`, `dash_dir`. On dash input (when cd<=0 and not sneaking): set dash_dir from current input vector, dash_t to ~0.18s, grant inv_t for ~0.16s (i-frames), boost speed to ~3.2x for the duration, set cd to ~0.55s (tunable, longer on harder difficulty). Movement during dash ignores blockers list (slip past enemies) but still respects walls via the existing per-axis collision, so you can't phase through level geometry. A motion-trail (2-3 fading ghost sprites) and a soft 'whoosh' synth sound sell it. Dashing into a wall cancels early. Optional upgrade key-item 'Phase Step' adds a second charge.
- **Difficulty impact:** The dodge is the answer to the difficulty spike: bullet-dodging arenas and boss slams are tuned around the i-frame window and cooldown, so reading the telegraph and timing the dash is the core challenge. Cooldown length is a difficulty lever — punishing players who panic-dash and reload-spam.
- **Implementation:** Implement in entities.py Player: in `move()`, if dash_t>0 apply dash_dir*dash_speed and decrement dash_t; manage cd in player.update(dt). Reuse the existing `_axis` wall collision; pass blockers=None during dash frames to allow passing enemies. Trail: push (x,y,dir,frame) into a small ringbuffer each dash frame; draw them with decreasing alpha before the main sprite in Player.draw. Add 'dash' sound to assets.Audio synth (short rising tri/noise blip). Add a cooldown pip to the HUD next to HP. Add the dash key to on_key (KEYDOWN, edge-triggered, only in 'play' and not during overlays). Serialize dash upgrade flags in the save bundle.
- **Theme tie:** A scared person's instinct is to flee — the dash literalizes flinch-and-escape. Tying its i-frames to survival keeps with 'survival is morally messy': you live by dodging rather than fighting, and the game notices whether you escape encounters or end them.

### Gun/Weapon Inventory & Projectile Combat

Serialize unlocked weapons, equipped id, and per-type ammo counts. Enemy HP and shot damage all route through the difficulty multipliers.
- **Theme tie:** A weapon in a scared person's hands is the theme made interactive: 'power changes people'. The game tracks lethal kills toward CRUELTY and non-lethal/Pacify clears toward MERCY, so HOW you fight — not just whether you win — shapes your identity, dialogue, trust, and endings. Choosing the unarmed slot in front of NPCs builds trust; brandishing builds fear.

### Run-Away Chases & Dodge Gauntlets
- **Purpose:** Deliver the requested run-away/dodge sequences as set-pieces: scripted high-tension escapes (flee Sufflok's collapsing theater, sprint the nightmare's lying corridors) and survive-the-pattern gauntlets that test the dash and movement, not just combat.
- **Controls:** Movement (WASD/Arrows) + dash (Space) are the whole toolkit. In a chase, you flee toward an exit while a pursuer/wall closes from behind and hazards/projectiles spawn ahead — getting touched costs HP and ground. In a gauntlet, you survive a timed barrage of telegraphed projectile/hazard waves in an arena, dashing through gaps until a 'survive' timer or wave count completes, opening the exit.
- **Design:** Two reusable modes layered on the play loop. CHASE: a `chaser` advances at a relentless speed (or a kill-wall scrolls), the camera biases forward, and a sequence of spawn-triggers (tied to player x/y progress) drop dodge obstacles — falling rubble (collapsing tiles), swinging hazards, gaps to dash across. Failing (caught or HP=0) reloads from the autosave at the chase entrance — not a checkpoint, so the run-up has stakes but isn't a whole-zone loss. GAUNTLET: a closed arena where a pattern director emits projectile waves (rings, aimed shots, sweeping lines, safe-gap walls) escalating over ~3 phases; a survive-timer/HP-threshold ends it. Both are data-described as a list of timed events so new ones are cheap to author. Variants per zone: theater (spotlights as moving damage cones — funny-on-surface, deadly underneath), prison (searchlights + spike floors), nightmare (looping doors where the wrong exit loops you back, the right one is found by reading a tell).
- **Difficulty impact:** These are the pure-skill peaks of the difficulty curve, balanced entirely around the dash i-frame window and cooldown. The pattern director's escalating waves and the chaser's speed are difficulty-table values. Because failure reloads the autosave at the sequence start (cheap retry, expensive run-up), they're tense but not rage-inducing.
- **Implementation:** Add a SequenceDirector helper (in game.py or a new sequences.py): holds a timeline of events [(t, fn)] plus per-frame spawners; update() advances time, fires events, spawns Projectiles/hazards, checks win/lose. Reuse the Projectile system for gauntlet bullets and the collapsing/spike tile props for chase hazards. Chaser is a Guard-like actor with a fixed lead speed and an instant-grab radius -> player.hurt or fail. Camera forward-bias: offset the existing _update_camera target by velocity. Lose -> start_fade -> apply_save(auto.json) and reposition at the sequence's entry. Author sequences as data so the nightmare 'lying doors' is just a transition table where most exits loop to the same room until a flag (read a sign / solve a riddle) is set. Telegraph every wave (0.3s warning marker) to stay fair.
- **Theme tie:** Fleeing is the scared outsider's signature verb — these sequences are 'fear in motion'. The theater chase weaponizes forced-loyalty spectacle (spotlights that hunt you) so the 'funny-on-surface, humiliating underneath' tone is mechanical. The nightmare's lying doors literalize 'doors that lie' and the portal mystery, rewarding attention over force.

### Upgraded Stealth
- **Purpose:** Evolve the existing raycast-cone stealth from a one-zone gimmick into a deep, recurring system (prison and castle infiltration) with more tools, smarter guards, and real consequences — so stealth stays a viable, distinct playstyle in the harder game.
- **Controls:** Shift = sneak (slower, quieter, smaller noise radius). F = throw a Noise Bell (now a limited item) to lure. New: a CROUCH-behind-cover read where standing adjacent to cover tiles further reduces detection; 'Smoke Pinch' (item) instantly breaks line-of-sight; takedown prompt (Z) when directly behind an unaware guard performs a quiet non-lethal knockout (mercy) or lethal (cruelty) takedown. Light/shadow: staying in shadow tiles lowers detection; stepping into lit tiles raises it.
- **Design:** Extend the Guard FSM with memory and senses: a 'last known position' the guard searches (already partially present via investigate), a hearing radius that reacts to running/dashing/gunfire (sprinting near a guard pings it; firing a gun is loud and pulls everyone), and graduated alert states (calm -> suspicious -> searching -> alerted) with a visible state icon. Add cover-snapping and a light grid: new tile props for 'shadow' (detection mult <1) and 'lit' (mult >1), and movable light cones from lamps/searchlights in the prison. Detection meter and reinforcement timers integrate with the difficulty table. Takedowns: when behind an unaware guard within range and facing same dir, Z removes them (knockout = MERCY+, they wake later and raise alarm if found; kill = CRUELTY+, permanent but bloodier and noisier if witnessed). Failure now means damage/combat (HP system) rather than a free note respawn, with autosave as the fallback.
- **Difficulty impact:** Smarter memory + hearing + reinforcements make pure ghosting hard and rewarding; the louder the tool, the riskier (guns trade stealth for power). Light/shadow adds positional puzzle-pressure. Because detection feeds the HP/combat systems on failure, a blown stealth run becomes a desperate fight or flee — raising stakes without a dead-end.
- **Implementation:** Build on the existing Guard.sees() and modes. Add `alert_level` (0-3) with thresholds and a per-guard `last_seen` point; on losing sight, walk to last_seen then sweep (extend current 'investigate'/'look'). Hearing: in _update_stealth, if player is sprinting/dashing/firing within a radius, raise that guard's suspicion. Light grid: add tile props 's' (shadow) and 'l' (lit) to world.py PROPS with a `light_mult` lookup used in detection math; searchlights are moving cones reusing draw_cone with a position the director animates. Takedown: detect 'behind + same facing + range' in try_interact and branch on chosen lethality (consume nothing, apply moral, set a 'body found' timer that can re-alert). Gunfire noise hooks into the weapon system (fire -> ping nearby guards). Serialize knocked-out/dead guard ids per zone in the save bundle so reloads are consistent. Keep the existing detection HUD bar; add the per-guard alert icon (reuse the !/? marks).
- **Theme tie:** Stealth is mercy's natural ally — ghosting past or knocking out spares lives and builds trust, while lethal takedowns and loud guns feed cruelty and fear. The choice of knockout vs kill at the exact same prompt is the cleanest expression of 'mercy is active, not passive': sparing costs you (they may wake and hunt you), so kindness is a deliberate, risky verb.

### Connective Systems: Feedback, Status Effects, Intimidation, Recruit/Spare
- **Purpose:** The cross-cutting 'glue' that makes every other system feel good and ties all mechanics back to the Mercy/Survival/Cruelty theme: hit feedback and juice, status effects for depth, cruelty-driven intimidation that makes enemies flee, and mercy-driven sparing/recruiting of bosses and enemies.
- **Controls:** Mostly emergent from other systems, but with theme-specific verbs: an INTIMIDATE prompt (Z) appears when a CRUEL-leaning player faces a low-HP or fearful enemy — they flee or surrender instead of fighting. A SPARE prompt (Z) appears when a MERCY-leaning player has reduced a boss/enemy to its 'broken' threshold without lethal finishers — choosing it ends the fight non-lethally and may RECRUIT the foe (they aid you later, open a door, or join as a follower). Status effects are applied by weapons/hazards (stun, slow, burn) and shown as small icons over actors.
- **Design:** FEEDBACK/JUICE: centralized helpers — screenshake (already specced), hitstop (freeze update for ~40-60ms on heavy hits/boss slams), damage-number/flash popups, particle bursts (reuse the mote/dust primitives), directional knockback, and per-event synth sounds. STATUS EFFECTS: a small list of (type, ttl, magnitude) on any actor — 'stun' (no act), 'slow' (speed mult), 'burn' (DoT), 'fear' (flees), 'shield' (absorb) — ticked each frame, drawn as icons. INTIMIDATION: cruelty score + recent lethal actions raise an enemy's 'fear'; past a threshold (or via the Coil's stun + a cruel reputation) weaker enemies surrender/flee on the INTIMIDATE prompt, granting fast clears but cementing the cruel path and lowering Rook's/villagers' trust. RECRUIT/SPARE: bosses have a non-lethal 'broken' state (reached by Pacify darts, knockouts, or simply not landing the killing blow); SPARE resolves the fight, awards MERCY, and can flip the foe to an ally NPC (e.g. a spared elite guard later unlocks a gate, a spared Riddle Creature gives a portal clue, a spared mini-boss fights beside you). RELATIONSHIP meters (rook_respect, ankam_arc, villager_trust, shopkeeper) shift from these choices and gate dialogue, prices, available paths, and endings.
- **Difficulty impact:** Hitstop/shake/telegraphs make hard fights readable and satisfying (fair difficulty). Status effects add tactical layers (stun-lock a charging elite, burn a boss between dodges). Intimidation is a high-risk SHORTCUT — it trivializes some fights but locks you onto the punishing cruel-world social track (worse trust, fewer allies, Sufflok takes interest in you as a rival). Sparing/recruiting is the harder, slower, resource-costlier route that pays off with allies who make later zones easier — so the moral choice has real mechanical weight both ways.
- **Implementation:** Add `self.shake`, `self.hitstop_t` to Game (hitstop: if >0, skip world simulation but keep drawing for a few frames). Add a generic `statuses` list to actors with a tick in their update. Reuse mote/dust draw code for particle bursts; add a `ui.draw_status_icons` and `ui.draw_damage_popup`. Intimidation/Spare prompts are context branches in try_interact gated on `self.moral` dominance, the target's hp/'broken' flag, and recent-kill tracking. Recruit flips an enemy's kind to 'ally' and adds an id to a `recruited` set in the save bundle; ally behavior reuses NPC.move/move_actor and simple follow logic. Relationship meters are plain ints in run state, serialized in the save bundle, and read by dialogue/shop/ending selectors. All damage/heal/fear thresholds read the difficulty table. Add synth one-shots: 'intimidate' (low menacing tone), 'spare' (warm resolve chord, reuse 'save'-like motif), 'stun', 'burn_tick'.
- **Theme tie:** This is the theme engine. 'Mercy is active, not passive' = SPARE/recruit costs effort and resources but builds a network of allies; 'cruelty works too fast' = INTIMIDATE is a real, tempting shortcut that the world punishes socially and that makes Sufflok see you as a kindred rival; 'power changes people' = the same gun and the same prompt produce opposite identities depending on how you wield them. Every connective effect ultimately routes into the moral meters that drive trust, fear, available paths, and the game's multiple endings.

### THE TAX-WAGON & THE COLLECTOR  (Act 1)
*Arena: A long, narrow ruined-village road segment (a wider variant of scene_fall's cracked 'P' road) walled by 'T' tree rows and 'r' rubble so the player cannot leave the lane. ### THE RIDDLE-KING (a.k.a. THE DOOR THAT LIES)  (Act 5)

The finale's THESIS: he is what the player could become on the cruelty path, and what mercy refuses to become. His cruelty has STRUCTURE — every attack is orderly, rhythmic, bureaucratic-made-deadly. **Mechanic taught:** Culmination boss: rhythmic grid BULLET-HELL, decree wall-with-gap reading, drifting-platform physics, shrinking-arena endurance, and the payoff of EVERY prior mechanic (dash i-frames, projectile dodging, ally-gaps from puzzles solved). **Phases:**

- *Phase 3 - The Bare Throne (fear, unarmored)* (33% -> 0% (or non-combat resolution)): The grand patterns collapse into a small shrinking circle of light; Sufflok fights desperate and personal — fast erratic radial bursts, nothing orderly left, mirroring a frightened man.; He drains the arena to a tiny safe disc, a final endurance bullet-dodge, while monologuing his real fear of the portals/outsiders.; A last 'all-in' decree: one massive screen-filling pattern with a single survivable path determined by the player's accumulated allies (freed prisoners/Riddle-guide/Ankam open gaps).

- **Survival:** OUTLAST & ESCAPE (don't beat him — leave): A survival player's goal was always HOME, not the throne. They survive his phases to reach the portal mechanism behind the throne and ESCAPE through it mid-fight, refusing the duel entirely. Sufflok is left intact, ranting, his structure unbroken but the outsider gone — the one variable he could never file. Ambiguous, morally messy ending: the player got out, the world did not get saved. +Survival. Tests pure endurance + a final escape-dash gauntlet (chase-boss DNA returns at the very end).
- **Cruelty:** TAKE THE THRONE (overpower, with the ultimate cost): A cruel player beats Sufflok to 0. The portal can be opened, but the player leaves (or stays) having become the thing they feared; the world swaps one tyrant for another, and the people who flinched from you were right. ## Design Review (critique)

**Strengths:**
- MORAL SYSTEM IS LOAD-BEARING AND DIEGETIC, NOT BOLTED ON. The three leanings are wired into mechanics that already exist in the engine (the Guard FSM, the choice box, the moral dict in game.py line 47). The strongest design instinct in the whole package is making HOW you fight register morally: lethal vs non-lethal takedowns at the SAME Z-prompt, unarmed-slot building trust, the Pacify dart. This is the real Undertale-adjacent identity and it is feasible — it reuses the existing moral counters and dialogue tables. Protect this above everything.
- THE BOSS DESIGN PHILOSOPHY (bosses that fight BY SYSTEM, not by hand) is genuinely smart and engine-honest. The Herald conducts waves, the Warden operates panels, Sufflok signs decrees — none require new melee-collision tech the engine lacks; they compose from projectiles + the existing cone/raycast + tile hazards. The three-path resolution per boss (expose/disable/upstage) ties combat to theme cleanly. This is the most reusable, most distinctive part of the package.
- THE DASH-WITH-I-FRAMES + PROJECTILE PILLAR IS THE RIGHT CORE VERB and it slots into the existing per-axis collision (Player._axis, entities.py line 83) and the y-sorted draw_list (game.py line 1015) with minimal surgery. The doc even correctly flags the y-sort insertion point for projectiles — that is the kind of integration-aware note that de-risks a single integrator.
- AUTOSAVE-ON-TRANSITION AS THE FAIRNESS VALVE is the correct answer to 'make it punishing.' Capping max loss at one zone keeps tension high without rage. The save bundle is specified as plain JSON of run state (no Surfaces) which is exactly right and already half-built in save.py.
- THE ART CRITIQUE IS UNUSUALLY CONCRETE AND CORRECT. The hair bald-spot fix, the 1px outline for readability, per-zone ambient-multiply, and the muzzle/hazard y-sort warning are real, specific, cheap wins grounded in the actual assets.py/_draw_character code. This section could be handed to an integrator almost verbatim.
- PORTAL MYSTERY AS THEMATIC SPINE (Sufflok is an outsider who came through a door and never went home) gives the 6-act structure a real reason to exist and pays off the 'why you, why now' curiosity hook. The echo-rooms idea (your past choices become walls or bridges) is the single most original mechanic in the doc and is the literal embodiment of the moral system.

**Gaps:**
- NO VERTICAL SLICE IS NAMED. The package is a 6-act content bible, but there is no decision about what to build FIRST to prove the new core loop (dash + projectile + HP + save + one boss) is fun. Everything is specced at equal priority. For a single integrator this is the most dangerous omission: it invites building breadth (zones, sprites, lore) before the moment-to-moment combat is proven enjoyable. The doc never carves out 'Act One retuned + the Tax-Wagon chase boss' as a provable slice.
- THE EXISTING save.py IS IGNORED. The Systems doc proposes writing saves.py from scratch with a different path scheme (~/.jun_world/saves, slot0/1/2 + auto) while the repo already has jun_world/save.py with a working SaveManager (slots auto/1/2/3, slot_%s.json). This is a real disconnect — the designer wrote the spec without reading the code. It signals other 'reuse the existing X' claims may be aspirational rather than verified.
- TEACHING CURVE FOR THE NEW COMBAT IS UNDER-DESIGNED. The game's entire existing identity is forgiving stealth with an infinite F=noise. Act One now must teach, in one act: HP, stamina, dash i-frames, aimed sling, save slots, game-over, AND a chase boss with projectile-dodging. That is 6+ new literacies stacked onto a tutorial that currently teaches 'move and talk.' There is no isolated safe-practice space for the dash before the Tax-Wagon boss demands it under pressure. New players will hit the difficulty wall before they own the verbs.
- MERCY IS SYSTEMICALLY PUNISHED, WHICH FIGHTS THE THESIS. Across the package: sparing forgoes loot drops (resource scarcity bites), knockouts can wake and re-alarm, recruiting costs time/resources, the cruelty intimidate-prompt is repeatedly called a 'fast shortcut that trivializes fights.' Under hard resource scarcity, the optimizing player learns cruelty is cheaper and safer NOW and mercy is a deferred-payoff gamble. The doc asserts mercy pays off with allies later, but 'later' loses to 'now' for most players. The theme 'mercy is active' risks becoming 'mercy is the masochist's difficulty mode.'
- TELEGRAPHS-THAT-LIE (Act 5 Riddle-King) DIRECTLY UNDERMINES THE GAME'S OWN FAIRNESS CONTRACT. The whole difficulty system is built on a promise: every threat is telegraphed (0.25s wind-up flash, no off-screen kills). Act 5 then inverts that promise as a 'central trick.' That is clever on paper but in practice reads as the game cheating after 4 acts of teaching trust. It needs an ironclad, learnable meta-rule (the honest cue) introduced BEFORE damage is on the line, or it will feel like a betrayal, not a twist.
- ESCORT, RHYTHM-GAUNTLET, NON-EUCLIDEAN LOOPING, AND CIRCUIT/XOR PUZZLES ARE EACH SPECCED AS IF TRIVIAL. These are the four highest-risk, highest-bug-surface features (panicking-NPC pathfinding/state, frame-accurate beat timing in a dt loop, room-relabeling logic that must never softlock, lights-out parity that must always be solvable). None has a fallback or anti-softlock guarantee beyond a sentence. For a single shipper, any one of these can eat weeks.
- CHARACTER ECONOMY IS THIN AGAINST THE RUNTIME. A 'pretty long' 6-act game leans on FOUR recurring NPCs (Rook, Ankam, Shopkeeper, Servant) plus a Riddle Creature. Rook in particular is asked to react meaningfully to 3 moral paths across 6 acts — that is dozens of dialogue states, none of which are scripted here. The relationship meters are specced; the content that makes them matter is not, and that content is where 'good story' actually lives or dies.

**Risks:**
- SCOPE-TO-INTEGRATOR RATIO IS THE EXISTENTIAL RISK. The current repo is ~2,800 lines for ONE act with 3 tiny scenes and a forgiving loop. The package specifies 6 acts, 5 multi-phase bosses, 14 puzzles, ~10 systems, ~30 new sprites/effects, 6 full tilesets and palettes — each act a content set larger than the entire current game. For 'a single integrator,' this is plausibly a 10-20x codebase expansion. The realistic failure mode is not a bad game; it is an unfinished one abandoned in Act 3. Nothing in the package is staged to ship incrementally as a complete experience at each step.
- DIFFICULTY-BY-MULTIPLIER-TABLE CREATES A BALANCE BLACK HOLE. Centralizing tunables is good engineering, but the package has zero concrete numbers (damage values, i-frame windows in ms, cooldowns, ammo counts, detect rates). 'Harder but fair' is asserted, never specified. A single integrator with no playtest team will not find the fun-vs-frustration line for 5 bosses and 6 chase/gauntlet sequences by intuition. Without a target like 'unaware player dies in 3 hits, careful player clears boss phase 1 in ~4 dodge windows,' balance will swing between trivial and rage.
- THE 'LYING DOORS' AND LOOPING-ROOM LOGIC IS A SOFTLOCK FACTORY. Doors-that-lie + dread meter + room relabeling per loop, all of which must (a) always have a discoverable exit, (b) never trap the player with no information, and (c) survive save/load mid-loop with the loop-counter intact. The save bundle must serialize transient puzzle state correctly or reloading drops the player into an inconsistent maze. This is the feature most likely to ship broken.
- ENEMY-AOE-HURTS-NEUTRALS (Warden fight, Act 3) IS A FRUSTRATION TRAP, not a moral test, unless friendly-fire is extremely legible. Asking the player to protect wandering neutral 'villager' sprites from their own ricochet/AoE during a bullet-dodging boss — with permanent moral consequences — punishes the player for the chaos the boss itself creates. High chance players feel cheated by a mercy-loss they didn't intend.
- RHYTHM/TIMING GAUNTLET (Act 4) ASSUMES BEAT-ACCURATE INPUT IN A VARIABLE-dt LOOP WITH PROCEDURAL AUDIO. Frame-timing music cues without an audio asset pipeline, on a clock.tick loop, is technically the riskiest mechanic in the package. If the beat and the visuals drift even slightly, the act's entire signature mechanic feels unfair. There is no fallback if rhythm-sync proves infeasible.
- STEALTH BECOMES INCOHERENT ONCE GUNS EXIST. The package makes firing 'loud — pulls everyone,' but also makes projectile combat a core pillar and ammo scarce. In practice players will default to whichever the level rewards, and the two systems (ghost vs gunfight) will constantly step on each other unless each zone hard-commits to one. The doc wants both available everywhere, which usually yields a muddy 'shoot until cornered, then there's no stealth left' loop.

**Fun factors:**
- MOMENT-TO-MOMENT FUN LIVES IN THE DASH-THROUGH-THE-GAP. The single most fun verb on offer is timing an i-frame dash through a telegraphed volley and coming out the other side — this is the 'feel-good' core and the whole game should be built outward from making THAT feel crunchy (screenshake, dash trail, hitstop, a satisfying whoosh). If the dash feels great, the hard difficulty becomes a pleasure; if it feels mushy, everything else collapses.
- THE SAME-PROMPT MORAL FORK (Z = knockout or kill behind an unaware guard) is fun because it makes the player author their identity in a split-second under pressure, not in a menu. That micro-decision, repeated, is the experiential heart of the moral system and is more powerful than any dialogue choice.
- SYSTEM-BOSSES THAT YOU OUT-THINK RATHER THAN OUT-DPS (expose the Herald's script, black out the Warden's panels, unwire Sufflok's ledger) are fun because they reward reading the arena, not grinding HP. Each gives a 'puzzle inside a dodge-fight' that is more memorable than a damage race.
- INTIMIDATE-MAKES-ENEMIES-FLEE is viscerally fun as a power fantasy and is the rare mechanic where the cruel path FEELS different to play, not just reads different in text. The danger is it's TOO fun/easy (see risks) — but the core sensation of an arena emptying because they're scared of you is a strong, distinct hook.
- ECHO ROOMS (your past saved/frightened characters become bridges or walls) is the most surprising and emotionally resonant idea in the package — the moment a player realizes the dungeon is built from their own choices is the kind of 'productive model-break' that creates the emotional weirdness the bible is chasing.
- RESOURCE-SCARCITY PANIC (rationing the last heal, deciding whether to spend ammo or run) is a reliable tension engine that turns the harder difficulty from a chore into a series of small gambling decisions — IF the economy is tuned so the player is usually one bad fight from trouble, never softlocked broke.

**Pacing:** "The ACT-TO-ACT MECHANIC CURVE is well-conceived on paper but front-loaded too steeply and back-loaded too ambitiously. Act 1 introduces too much at once (HP, stamina, dash, sling, saves, game-over, a chase boss) for a game whose current tutorial teaches only movement — that is the steepest part of the curve placed at the moment of lowest player skill. Acts 2-3 (projectiles+economy, then stealth-systems+escort) are a sensible escalation and the strongest stretch of the curve. Acts 4-5 then spike into the two most technically risky mechanics (rhythm-timing, non-Euclidean looping) right where a fatiguing player most needs reliability, not novelty — and Act 4's 'forced theater' tonal shift risks reading as filler between the prison's systems-peak and the Underground's mystery-payoff. The INTENSITY curve (each act ends on a boss that synthesizes its zone's mechanic) is genuinely good structure. The fix is not to reorder but to (1) flatten Act 1's onboarding so verbs arrive one at a time with practice space, (2) swap Act 4's highest-risk mechanic for a dt-safe memory variant so the late game stays reliable, and (3) ensure each act is independently shippable so pacing can be validated act-by-act rather than discovered only after all six are built. As written, the curve's quality cannot be confirmed until the whole game exists — which is exactly the wrong time to learn your pacing is off."

**Top recommendations:**
- **[MUST] Carve a vertical slice: retuned Act One + the Tax-Wagon chase boss. Build and prove THAT before any new zone.** — _why:_ The package's fatal risk is scope abandonment. The new core loop (dash i-frames + HP/death + save-or-reload + one projectile-dodge boss + the moral intimidate/spare verbs) is entirely unproven and is the make-or-break of the whole pivot. If that 30-minute slice is not fun, no amount of Act 5 lore saves it. If it IS fun, the rest is content authoring against a proven core. _how:_ Scope the slice to exactly: HP+i-frames on Player (entities.py), dash bound to a new key (NOT Shift — keep sneak), the Sling as one projectile type reusing the y-sorted draw_list, wire the EXISTING save.py (do not rewrite it) for autosave-on-_do_transition and a Game Over -> load path, and the Tax-Wagon as a scrolling-lane chase using existing collapsing-tile-as-hazard. Cut everything else from the first build. Playtest THIS to the fun bar before authoring Act Two.
- **[MUST] Add an isolated dash/dodge practice beat before any boss demands the verb, and put concrete numbers in the difficulty table.** — _why:_ The game today teaches 'move and talk.' You are stacking 6 new literacies onto Act One and then immediately gating progress on a projectile-dodge boss. Players will fail the verb they were never given room to own, and 'harder but fair' is currently an assertion with no target values to balance against. _how:_ Insert a no-stakes corridor where Rook tosses harmless pebbles you must dash through (i-frames teach themselves with zero death). Then write the actual numbers into S.DIFFICULTY: i-frame window in ms, dash cooldown, 'unaware player dies in N hits,' 'boss phase 1 clears in ~M dodge windows.' Tune the slice to those targets so the rest of the game has a calibrated reference.
- **[MUST] Fix the mercy-is-punished incentive: make non-lethal/spare carry an IMMEDIATE, legible reward, not only a deferred one.** — _why:_ Behavior over intention: under hard scarcity, an optimizing player learns cruelty is cheaper and safer right now while mercy is a gamble that pays off acts later. That trains the opposite of your thesis. 'Mercy is active' must not collapse into 'mercy is the hard mode for people who read the lore.' _how:_ Give mercy a here-and-now mechanical payout that competes with the cruelty shortcut: e.g. spared/recruited enemies drop MORE resources than killed ones (sparing as the resource-rich route, not the poor one), knockouts grant a brief safe window, and unarmed-in-front-of-NPCs unlocks shop discounts or free items immediately. Keep cruelty fast but make it spend something the player feels instantly (allies refuse to trade, prices spike now). Test by watching whether a player who wants to optimize drifts mercy or cruelty.
- **[SHOULD] Cut or radically de-risk the four highest-bug features for v1: full escort AI, frame-accurate rhythm, non-Euclidean looping, and friendly-fire moral tracking.** — _why:_ Each is a multi-week, softlock-prone, hard-to-balance system for a single shipper, and none is required to prove the core is fun. They are where an abandoned project dies. Elegance beats accumulation: the game can be excellent with stealth, projectiles, dashes, switch/sokoban puzzles, and system-bosses alone. _how:_ DEFER escort to a scripted 'follow-the-leader on a fixed path that pauses when you stop' (no panic-AI). DEFER rhythm-gauntlet; reskin Act 4's signature as a pattern-MEMORY (Simon-says, which you already spec and which is dt-safe) rather than beat-timing. DEFER non-Euclidean looping to a simpler 'doors-that-lie' with a single fixed meta-rule and a guaranteed-visible fallback exit after N loops. CUT enemy-AoE-hurts-neutrals as a moral consequence — keep neutrals as obstacles only. Add these back post-ship if the core lands.
- **[SHOULD] Resolve the stealth-vs-guns coherence per zone: each zone hard-commits to one dominant pillar.** — _why:_ Making firing 'loud' while also making projectile combat a core pillar and ammo scarce yields a muddy loop where neither system is satisfying. Players need to know what game they're playing in each room. _how:_ Designate stealth-primary zones (prison, castle infiltration) where guns are a last-resort panic button that escalates to a fight-or-flee fail-state, and combat-primary zones (court arena, theater finale, bosses) where stealth is irrelevant. Make the transition diegetic (alarm = you're now in the gun game). Do not offer both as co-equal everywhere.
- **[SHOULD] Introduce the Act 5 'honest cue' meta-rule early and harmlessly, so the telegraph inversion reads as a twist, not a cheat.** — _why:_ Four acts teach the player to trust telegraphs; Act 5 inverts them as a 'central trick.' Without a learnable, fair rule taught before damage is at stake, this breaks the fairness contract the whole difficulty system rests on and feels like the game lying to the player, not the world lying to the character. _how:_ Seed the honest signal (the lantern-lit true door / the pulse-in-time-with-the-hum) in a zero-stakes room at the Underground entrance, with the Riddle Creature explicitly naming the rule once. Only after the player has used it safely do you put it under threat. Keep the THREAT un-masterable (preserve dread) but keep the RULE fair.
- **[STRETCH] Compress to a shippable spine first: prove 3 acts end-to-end before committing to 6.** — _why:_ A finished 3-act game is infinitely better than an abandoned 6-act one. The package's own structure (Villages -> Kingdom -> Prison, then the portal-reveal Underground -> Castle) can land a complete emotional arc at Act 3's ledger-revelation if needed, with Theater/Underground as expansion. _how:_ Define a 'minimum complete game' = Acts 1-3 plus a truncated finale that still delivers the portal reveal and at least one ending per moral path. Build that to ship-quality. Treat Acts 4-5 (Theater, full Underground) as a stretch expansion gated on the first three landing. This makes every milestone a complete, releasable experience.
